Offline Survival Guidelines

100% Offline PDF-style

Critical procedures to keep you alive when communication networks are disabled.

🩺 Emergency First Aid Measures

1. Heavy Bleeding Control:

Apply firm, direct pressure with a clean cloth. Elevate the wound above the heart. Do not remove dressing if soaked; pile more cloth on top.

2. Respiration Failure (CPR):

30 chest compressions (2 inches deep, rate 100-120/min) followed by 2 rescue breaths. Repeat until medical response arrives.

3. Heat Stroke Relief:

Move victim to shade. Cool them with water mist, wet clothes, and fan them. Give sips of cool water if fully conscious.

🌊 Flood Survival Guidance

1. Move to Higher Ground:

Evacuate immediately. Never stay in basements or lower floors. Do not cross rushing water paths.

2. Electrical Isolation:

Turn off main grid switches inside your dwelling if water enters. Never touch live electrical items when wet.

3. Water Safety:

Do not drink tap water. Drink boiled or purified water only. Treat any skin contact with sanitizer if possible.

🏢 Earthquake - Drop, Cover, Hold On

1. Inside a Building:

DROP to your hands and knees. COVER your head/neck under a sturdy table or desk. HOLD ON until shaking stops.

2. Outdoors:

Move to clear open fields away from power lines, glass facades, buildings, and flyovers. Drop low to avoid falling.

3. Trapped Under Rubble:

Do not light matches or kick up dust. Tap on pipes/walls (or activate our Morse-SOS beacon app). Shout only as a last resort to preserve energy.

🌀 Cyclone Protection Steps

1. Secure Windows & Openings:

Tape windows, board them up, and seal door cracks. Stay in the inner-most room away from outside glass panels.

2. Keep Emergency Kit Nearby:

Keep batteries, dry rations, first aid, radios, and power banks wrapped in waterproof bags.

3. Avoid Eye of Cyclone Delusion:

If winds suddenly calm, the cyclone center is passing. Stay inside. Heavy destructive winds will resume in the opposite direction quickly.
